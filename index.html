Planner Insight

With 120 seats, 12 blocked / dedicated seats, 24 HOOP hours, 7 operating days, and a 5-day work pattern, the current floor can support approximately 384 roster HC before shrinkage adjustment. After applying 30% shrinkage, effective productive support is around 269 HC. The recommended shift mix is 9h + 9h + 8h because it improves seat reuse, reduces idle seat hours, and keeps overlap more controlled than a tighter max-utilization design. Main improvement opportunity: moving from a standard 2-wave model to the recommended 3-wave structure can improve utilization from 148% to 201% and increase supportable roster HC without adding new seats.

Formula Glossary

Concurrent Seat Capacity

Total Seats minus blocked, dedicated, or non-production seats. Concurrent Seat Capacity = Total Seats - Dedicated / Blocked Seats

Available Seat Hours

Total seat-hours physically available in one operating day. Available Seat Hours = Concurrent Seats × HOOP Hours

Effective Seat Reuse Factor

Adjusted seat reuse after considering HOOP length, shift length, and reuse efficiency. Effective Seat Reuse Factor = (HOOP Hours ÷ Shift Length) × Reuse Efficiency

Roster HC Supported

Total weekly roster HC supportable from the same seat base. Roster HC Supported = Concurrent Seats × Effective Reuse Factor × (HOOP Days ÷ Working Days per Agent)

Effective Productive HC Supported

Roster HC support after shrinkage deduction. Productive HC Supported = Roster HC Supported × (1 - Shrinkage)

Seat Utilization %

Comparison of required seat load versus available seat-hours. Seat Utilization % = Required Seat Hours ÷ Available Seat Hours × 100

Required Seat Hours

Total scheduled seat load driven by staffing need. Required Seat Hours = Scheduled HC × Average Shift Length

Recommended Seats

Seat need including extra seats and planning buffer. Recommended Seats = (Peak Scheduled HC + Dedicated Extra Seats) × (1 + Buffer %)

Seat Turn Ratio

How many times one seat is effectively reused in the operating model. Seat Turn Ratio = HOOP Hours ÷ Shift Length (adjusted by reuse efficiency)

Overlap Waste

Seat-hours lost due to shift overlaps that do not create extra usable production capacity. Overlap Waste = Total Overlap Seat-Hours - Productive Reuse Value
